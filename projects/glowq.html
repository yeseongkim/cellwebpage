GlowQ: Group-Shared LOw-Rank Approximation for Quantized LLMs

GlowQ shares low-rank corrections across quantized transformer groups, reducing latency and memory overhead while preserving LLM quality.

Authors Selim An, Il Hong Suh, and Yeseong Kim

Abstract

What the paper is doing.

Published as a conference paper at ICLR 2026 G LOW Q: G ROUP -S HARED L OW-R ANK A PPROXIMA TION FOR Q UANTIZED LLM S Ilhong Suh COGA robotics, Korea ihsuh@coga-robotics.com arXiv:2603.25385v2 [cs.LG] 30 Apr 2026 Selim An Department of Artificial Intelligence DGIST, Korea phantom06@dgist.ac.kr Yeseong Kim Department of Electrical Engineering POSTECH, Korea yeseongkim@postech.ac.kr A BSTRACT Quantization techniques such as BitsAndBytes (Dettmers et al., 2022), AWQ (Lin et al., 2024), and GPTQ (Frantar et al., 2022) are widely used as a standard method in deploying large language models but often degrades accuracy when using lowbit representations, e.g., 4 bits. Low-rank correction methods (e.g., LQER (Zhang et al., 2024a), QERA (Zhang et al., 2024b), ASER (Zhao et al., 2025)) has been proposed to mitigate this issue, however, they restore all layers and insert errorcorrection modules into every decoder block, which increases latency and memory overhead. To address this limitation, we propose GlowQ, a group-shared low-rank approximation for quantized LLMs that caches a single shared right factor per input-sharing group and restores only the groups or layers that yield the highest accuracy benefit. GlowQ computes the high-precision projection once per inputsharing group and reuses it across its modules, reducing parameter and memory overhead, and retaining the expressivity of layer-specific corrections. We also propose a selective variant, GlowQ-S, that applies the cached shared module only where it provides the largest benefit.
