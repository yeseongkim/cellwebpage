NSHD keeps the feature quality of CNNs while moving the final learner into a more efficient symbolic space.

The paper proposes a neuro-symbolic pipeline where a pretrained CNN provides intermediate visual features, a learned manifold layer compresses them, and an HD model learns the final symbolic representation. Knowledge distillation transfers supervision from the full CNN into the HD learner, so the hybrid model does not throw away the information held in the dropped layers.

Abstract

The paper is really asking whether HD computing can inherit the perceptual strength of CNNs without inheriting their full deployment cost and opacity.

Standalone HD computing is attractive because it is highly parallel, symbolic, and hardware friendly, but it struggles on image tasks where high-level feature extraction matters. The paper highlights that gap directly: a strong non-linear HD encoder still reaches only 39.88% on CIFAR-10 and 19.7% on CIFAR-100, which is far from modern CNN quality.

NSHD answers that gap with a neuro-symbolic design. A pretrained CNN contributes intermediate visual features, a learned manifold layer compresses those features before HD encoding, and a knowledge-distillation procedure transfers information from the full teacher network into the HD learner. The resulting model stays much lighter and more interpretable than a full CNN while recovering much of the CNN's task quality.

Motivation

Three limitations of earlier HD vision pipelines motivate the redesign.

The paper is strongest when read as a correction to earlier “CNN features plus HD” hybrids that still leave too much accuracy on the table or too much computation in the feature path.

Pure HD learning does not extract strong visual features

Image classification is the failure case that exposes the ceiling of conventional HD encoders. CNNs learn high-level representations from layered convolutions, while vanilla HD pipelines still depend on much shallower feature formation.

Earlier CNN-to-HD hybrids discard too much teacher knowledge

Prior work often cuts off the CNN after several convolution layers and simply ignores everything after that. The paper argues that this throws away useful knowledge stored in the remaining layers and leaves the HD student undertrained.

Raw CNN feature maps are too expensive to encode directly

Intermediate convolution layers can output tens of thousands of features. If those are sent directly into HD random projection, encoding becomes the new bottleneck. The manifold learner is introduced precisely to compress that boundary without retraining the entire CNN.

Pipeline

NSHD is a four-stage bridge from neural perception to symbolic learning.

The page should make clear that NSHD is not just “CNN features fed into HD.” The manifold learner and the distillation procedure are what make the integration complete.

Step 01
Take an intermediate layer from a pretrained CNN
NSHD chooses an empirically strong intermediate layer from models such as VGG16, MobileNetV2, or EfficientNet. Earlier layers are cheaper and later layers are stronger, so the model explicitly trades off feature richness against cost rather than blindly using the whole CNN backbone.
Step 02
Compress the feature map with a learned manifold layer
The extracted convolution features are first pooled, then passed through a fully connected manifold learner that reduces the feature count to a much smaller F-hat before HD encoding. This is what keeps the random projection stage from exploding in parameters and multiply-accumulate work.
Step 03
Encode the compressed features into hypervectors and retrain with MASS
The compressed features are symbolized with random projection into a binary HD representation, then class hypervectors are retrained using Many-class Similarity Scaling. MASS updates each class according to class-wise similarity differences rather than a simple correct-versus-incorrect rule.
Step 04
Distill the teacher CNN's logits into the HD student
NSHD softens both the HD similarity outputs and the teacher CNN predictions, then combines one-hot supervision with distilled soft-label updates. This lets the HD model absorb knowledge from the layers that were removed from the deployment path instead of discarding them.
Step 05
Backpropagate decoded HD error into the manifold learner only
Rather than retraining the full CNN, the paper decodes HD class-error hypervectors back into the manifold-learner feature space and updates only that compression layer. This is the critical trick that makes the integration computationally reasonable.
Step 06
Deploy the split pipeline on GPU or FPGA
The unchanged pretrained CNN prefix runs on commodity accelerators, while the HD stages map naturally to common GPU execution or to FPGA logic. The paper implements both, using the DPU path on ZCU104 for the CNN prefix and a hardware-friendly HD tail.

Why the manifold learner matters as much as distillation

Without the manifold learner, the CNN-to-HD boundary is too expensive. NSHD would inherit a huge feature tensor, large model size, and heavy encoding overhead. The paper's efficiency claim depends on shrinking that interface before symbolic learning begins.

Why this is more than a hybrid classifier

The KD step makes the HD learner train against the full teacher distribution, not just the hard label. That is what turns the system into a real neuro-symbolic transfer pipeline instead of a loose two-stage cascade.

Results

The paper's main claim is that symbolic learning can recover near-CNN quality while being materially cheaper to serve.

The results are strongest when read across three axes at once: energy, model size and compute efficiency, and the accuracy recovered by distillation.

What the paper shows about the value of each major NSHD ingredient

Component Reported evidence Interpretation
NSHD vs VanillaHD The paper reports that vanilla HD-style image learning remains far below CNN quality, while NSHD reaches comparable or better results once the CNN feature path and distillation are introduced. The symbolic learner needs strong perceptual features; otherwise image classification remains the weak spot of HD computing.
Knowledge distillation In the detailed EfficientNet-B7 layer-7 study, KD improves accuracy by 7.39%, and the broader experiments show that KD consistently closes the gap between the CNN teacher and the HD student. The dropped CNN layers still matter, and distillation is how NSHD captures that information without deploying those layers at runtime.
Manifold learner The learned compression path cuts MACs by up to 34% and keeps NSHD smaller than the prior BaselineHD style integration. For example, the paper notes that a naive VGG16 layer-29 path would have reached 96.61 MB where NSHD stays at 69.05 MB. The manifold learner is doing real systems work. It is not cosmetic dimensionality reduction.
3K vs 10K dimensions The paper defaults to D = 3,000 and reports that most configurations do not need the old 10K convention to recover CNN-level quality. Moving from 10K to 3K reduces the HD-side parameters by 70%. NSHD narrows both the accuracy gap and the deployment cost at the same time, which is why the hybrid is compelling.

This paper broadens HD computing from sensor-style workloads to full vision pipelines

The earlier HD success cases were often on lower-dimensional or more structured inputs. NSHD shows how HD learning can stay relevant even when the front end must handle modern visual representation learning.

The energy story comes from cutting the network early but training the student well

If you cut the CNN early without distillation, quality falls. If you keep the whole CNN, efficiency gains disappear. NSHD works because the HD tail plus KD makes the earlier cut useful rather than merely cheaper.

Explainability is treated as a model property, not just a marketing label

The paper uses t-SNE projections of hypervectors to show how symbolic representations become more class-organized over retraining. That does not solve explainability in a full formal sense, but it does show why the authors see HD space as more interpretable than a raw CNN classifier head.

Takeaways

Why this page matters inside CELL's HD line.

  1. Point 01
    NSHD is one of the clearest project pages for CELL's neuro-symbolic direction. It shows that HD computing does not need to replace deep learning to be useful; it can absorb its strengths and then take over the efficient symbolic tail.
  2. Point 02
    For the website, the manifold learner and KD should be treated as co-equal contributions. One makes the hybrid efficient enough to matter, and the other makes it accurate enough to matter.
  3. Point 03
    Chronologically, this page also expands the site from pure HD systems into hybrid symbolic-neural learning, which makes the broader trajectory of the lab easier to understand.
